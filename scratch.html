All About Snowboarding

Snowboarding is a popular winter sport that involves descending a snow-covered slope while standing on a board attached to a rider's feet. It combines elements of skiing, surfing, and skateboarding, and has a rich history and culture.

  1. Three facts to know about snowboarding:
    • Snowboarding was invented in the 1960s by Sherman Poppen, who created a toy for his daughter by fastening two skis together.
    • The first snowboard competition was held in 1981 in Vermont, USA, and it helped to popularize the sport.
    • Snowboarding became an official Olympic sport in 1998 at the Nagano Winter Olympics.
      • Events included halfpipe, giant slalom, and snowboard cross.
      • Shaun White is arguably the most famous Olympic snowboarder in history.
